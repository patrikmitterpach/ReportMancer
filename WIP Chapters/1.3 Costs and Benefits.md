While basic logging and monitoring are a standard part of software development, there is much to be gained by implementing additional observability features, especially when it comes to the deployment and maintenance of the software. 

As the complexity of the software stack grows, so does the difficulty with running the code itself. While smaller teams may be able to cover their observability needs with a few man-hours of developer's time, larger teams may require dedicated personel or even whole teams, which are solely focused on making sure the software runs well, without needless waste and that deployment of new code is quick, with bugs being caught before making their way into the production software.
